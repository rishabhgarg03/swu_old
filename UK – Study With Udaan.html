WHY STUDY IN THE UK

Historically, Indian parents have sent their children to study in the UK. A degree from any affiliated institution in UK is highly regarded everywhere in the world. What’s more, as there are 1.4 million Indians living in the UK, Indian students will feel right at home.

REASONS TO STUDY IN UK

Globally famed universities:

UK institutions rank among the world’s best, and the qualifications are internationally valued and recognised. 4 of the top 10 universities in the world, including the first, are in the UK. There are also older, established universities such as Oxford and Cambridge. The UK has 19 universities with a 5-star teaching rating, higher than any other country.

Lower Cost:

The UK has a lower cost of education for the international student than the US. Further, a degree will take less time to complete in the UK than in other countries.

Range of Courses:

So wide is the range that In the UK, you can study everything from art direction to aeronautical engineering. ​Further, ​universities in the UK are renowned for having campuses with students from a diverse range of backgrounds.

TOP UNIVERSITIES IN UK

There are 71 universities in the UK which rank among the world’s best in the QS World University Rankings 2016-2017, including four in the global top 10, and a further 26 in the top 200. Here is the list of the top 10 universities in the UK.

HOW TO APPLY

UNDERGRADUATE COURSES

All applications to UK universities are made through Universities and Colleges Admissions Service (UCAS), a centralised body for admissions and placements, a year before the start date of the course. You can put your application to 5 different courses in universities specified by the UCAS;​ in order to do this, acceptance from the UCAS is mandatory. You have the freedom to exercise your choice here. For example, you can apply to 5 different universities for the same course or 5 different courses at the same university. Indian students can apply anytime between 1st September and 30th June in the year preceding the academic year you wish to take admission. For some courses, applications need to be submitted by as early as mid-October of the preceding year.

MASTERS IN UK

Unlike undergraduate programmes, there is no centralised application system for master’s degrees. This means you’ll have to apply to each university separately. You can get the admission forms from respective university websites. Most Masters in UK start in September. However, some UK universities may offer multiple start dates. Remember that the deadlines for application are typically earlier for Indian students than for UK/EU/EEA (European Economic Area) students.
